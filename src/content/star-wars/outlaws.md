I'm playing through **Star Wars Outlaws** — Massive's open-world take on the
scoundrel fantasy, set between *The Empire Strikes Back* and *Return of the Jedi*.
You play Kay Vess, a thief building a crew (and a debt) across the Outer Rim with
her merqaal companion, Nix. This page is my running notebook: what's working, what
I'd do differently, and the bits worth knowing before you start.

> Living document — I update it as I push deeper into the syndicates. Lore links
> point to [Wookieepedia](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Star_Wars:_Outlaws).

## First impressions

The vibe is the win: dusty cantinas, sabacc tables, and speeder runs across open
vistas all sell the "small-time crook in a big galaxy" pitch. It's less about being
the hero and more about angles, leverage, and getting paid.

- **Lean into stealth early.** Kay is fragile in open firefights at the start; the
  game rewards patience, Nix distractions, and clean takedowns.
- **Talk to Nix constantly.** He grabs loot, hits switches, and tags enemies — he's
  half your toolkit, not a pet.
- **Don't hoard credits.** Spend on blaster modules and gear that unlocks new
  approaches rather than saving for a rainy day.

## Syndicate reputation

The reputation system is the spine of the game. Four syndicates — Pyke, Hutt,
Crimson Dawn, and the Ashiga Clan — track how they feel about you, and almost every
job nudges those meters in opposite directions.

- Reputation gates vendors, areas, and mission branches, so check who you're about
  to anger **before** you commit to a contract.
- "Bad standing" isn't game over — it changes prices and patrol aggression. Sometimes
  burning one faction to please another is the correct play.
- Expert-tier gear and the best fences live behind **good** standing, so pick a
  primary ally for each region.

## Tips I wish I had on day one

- **Adrenaline / quick-draw** is your panic button in a shootout — keep an eye on the
  meter and tag multiple foes when it's full.
- **Speeder ramps and shortcuts** trivialize a lot of travel; explore on the bike,
  not on foot.
- **Slice everything.** The data spikes and lockpicking minigames are quick and
  almost always worth the detour for crafting parts.
- **Save before sabacc.** Enough said.
