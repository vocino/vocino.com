> **Starter notes.** My personal take on Sorcerer, written as a starting point. The pickups are what I reach for; I retune the spell list every run. Edit freely.

A **Draconic Bloodline** Sorcerer whose first job is being a **metamagic battery** — Twinned [[bg3:haste]] on the two best martials is often worth more than anything I cast myself — and whose second job is deleting clustered enemies with [[bg3:scorching-ray]] and [[bg3:fireball]].

## Why I run it

- **Twinned Haste** doubles the action economy of your carries from level 5.
- **Quickened** lets me nuke *and* reposition in the same turn.
- Draconic gives **free CON to AC** (unarmored) and a damage rider on my element.

## Feats I take

- Ability Improvement (Charisma) — DCs first, always.
- [[bg3:alert|Alert]] — going first on a blaster is huge.

## Spells & actions

- [[bg3:haste|Haste]] (Twinned) — the build's signature play.
- [[bg3:scorching-ray|Scorching Ray]] — best early single-target burn.
- [[bg3:fireball|Fireball]] — clustered packs evaporate.
- [[bg3:counterspell|Counterspell]] — shut down enemy casters.

## Gear by act

- **Act 1:** Anything that boosts spell save DC or initiative.
- **Act 2:** Robes that add damage to your element.
- **Act 3:** [[bg3:markoheshkir|Markoheshkir]] for a free elemental bonus and a daily empowered spell.

## How I play it

Turn one is almost always Twinned [[bg3:haste]] on the carries, then I retreat to high ground. After that I lead with [[bg3:fireball]] on packs or [[bg3:scorching-ray]] on a single threat, holding a slot for [[bg3:counterspell]] whenever an enemy mage shows up.

## Make it yours

Prefer control over damage? Take **Storm Sorcery** and lean on flight + repositioning, or splash a couple **Tempest Cleric** levels for a guaranteed-max lightning nuke. The metamagic core is what matters.
