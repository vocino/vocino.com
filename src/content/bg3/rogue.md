> **Starter notes.** This is my favorite Rogue, which is really a Rogue/Ranger multiclass. Shared as a starting point — adjust the split and feats to taste.

A **Thief Rogue / Gloomstalker Ranger** archer built entirely around the first round of combat. From stealth I get a surprise turn, Gloomstalker's extra attack, and the Thief's bonus action — that's three or four [[bg3:sharpshooter]] arrows before most enemies have acted, usually ending the fight before it starts.

## Why I run it

- **Round-one burst** so high it skips whole encounters.
- **Sharpshooter + high ground** turns the −5/+10 into a non-issue.
- Two bonus actions (Thief) means [[bg3:hunters-mark]] *and* a second attack.

## Feats I take

- [[bg3:sharpshooter|Sharpshooter]] — non-negotiable for this build.
- [[bg3:alert|Alert]] — guarantees you get the alpha strike.

## Spells & actions

- [[bg3:hunters-mark|Hunter's Mark]] — bonus-action damage rider on the target.
- [[bg3:pass-without-trace|Pass Without Trace]] — the whole party sets up in stealth.
- [[bg3:misty-step|Misty Step]] — reposition to high ground or out of melee.

## Gear by act

- **Act 1:** [[bg3:titanstring-bow|Titanstring Bow]] — adds your Strength elixir bonus to every arrow.
- **Act 2:** Gloves and arrows that add elemental or flat damage.
- **Act 3:** A bow with extra attacks or status arrows.

## How I play it

Scout ahead, cast [[bg3:pass-without-trace]], and creep the whole party into position. I open from stealth: surprise attack, Dread Ambusher attack, Extra Attack, then the Thief bonus-action shot — every one a [[bg3:sharpshooter]] hit from high ground. Most fights are over before the enemy initiative comes up.

## Make it yours

Want a melee assassin instead? **Assassin Rogue** with a finesse weapon keeps the round-one identity. Prefer pure ranger? Drop the Thief levels for **Hunter** and Volley. The Gloomstalker opening is the part I never give up.
