> **Starter notes.** This is the Cleric I run when I want the "support" slot to also delete something on command. The build is mostly habits. Death Domain just gives you better spikes when you need them.

I play this as a shield cleric in medium armor. The loop is simple: keep the party's HP floor up with an upcast [[bg3:aid]], and spend your real turns either controlling space ([[bg3:spirit-guardians]] / [[bg3:glyph-of-warding]]) or erasing a problem with [[bg3:inflict-wounds]]. Later, once you've stacked enough spell DC, [[bg3:hold-person]] becomes a real opener instead of a coin toss.

## Why I run it

- [[bg3:inflict-wounds]] is a clean answer to "that thing has to die now."
- Upcast [[bg3:aid]] after every long rest. It makes the party harder to knock over, which buys you time to cast.
- When your DC is high enough, [[bg3:hold-person]] turns bosses into target dummies.

## Feats I take

- Ability Improvement (Wisdom) - every good Cleric turn is a save/DC question.
- [[bg3:war-caster|War Caster]] - if you're running [[bg3:spirit-guardians]] or holds, this keeps the whole plan from falling apart.

## Spells & actions

- [[bg3:inflict-wounds|Inflict Wounds]] - your burst button. Upcast it when the target matters.
- [[bg3:aid|Aid]] - cast it after every long rest; upcast as slots grow.
- [[bg3:spirit-guardians|Spirit Guardians]] - turn it on when you can stand in the middle and not get deleted.
- [[bg3:glyph-of-warding|Glyph of Warding]] - flexible AoE for when you need to play from range.
- [[bg3:hold-person|Hold Person]] - late game, with DC gear, this is how you start fights.

## Gear by act

- **Act 1:** Defensive staples and free value. Cloak of Protection, Pearl of Power, and any shield that buys you more casts.
- **Act 2:** Adamantine Scale Mail if you're sick of random crits; Eversight Ring if blind is ruining your day; Ritual Dagger of Shar is a fun necrotic-flavored mainhand.
- **Act 3:** Start chasing spell reliability. Hood/Cloak of the Weave, a Spell Save DC ring, and a "casters should not miss" weapon like Rhapsody. If you're tired of dying, Amulet of Greater Health basically solves CON.

(If you don't have the exact pieces, don't sweat it. You're mostly trying to land spells more often and keep concentration up.)

## How I play it

After every long rest I cast an upcast [[bg3:aid]]. In fights I decide what kind of turn it is:

- **Burst turn:** walk up and upcast [[bg3:inflict-wounds]] into the priority target.
- **Control turn:** if my DC is good and the target is humanoid, [[bg3:hold-person]] and let the martials do the rest.
- **Map control turn:** [[bg3:spirit-guardians]] when I can safely be central, or [[bg3:glyph-of-warding]] when I can’t.

Also: set your reactions so Touch of Death actually triggers. And once you have Inescapable Destruction, necrotic plans stop bouncing off the stuff you'd expect.

## Make it yours

If you want to be more "classic Cleric", swap some burst into buffs and you're still fine. If you want to be more "death", lean harder into necrotic/undead picks and keep stacking spell DC so control isn't a coin flip.
