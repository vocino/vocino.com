> **Starter notes.** My default Fighter, written as a starting point. Maneuvers and feats are personal preference — swap them around.

A **Battle Master** two-hander who is the most dependable martial in the game. Three attacks become six on an Action Surge turn, and maneuvers like Trip Attack convert raw damage into hard control — a prone boss is a boss everyone else gets advantage on.

## Why I run it

- **Most attacks per turn** of any class, especially with Action Surge.
- **Maneuvers add control:** Trip (prone), Menacing (frightened), Riposte (free hits).
- The simplest build to keep alive — it just works in every fight.

## Feats I take

- [[bg3:great-weapon-master|Great Weapon Master]] — −5/+10 plus bonus-action attacks on kills.
- [[bg3:savage-attacker|Savage Attacker]] — quietly great on big two-handers.

## Spells & actions

Fighter is martial, so this is maneuvers and items rather than spells:

- **Trip Attack** — prone the target so the party piles on with advantage.
- **Menacing Attack** — frighten a caster to shut it down.
- **Riposte** — punish every enemy that misses you.

## Gear by act

- **Act 1:** The best two-handed weapon you can find; heavy armor.
- **Act 2:** [[bg3:phalar-aluve|Phalar Aluve]] (Shriek) for a flat damage rider.
- **Act 3:** A weapon with extra attacks or a strong on-hit effect.

## How I play it

If I'm not going first, I take the hit — this build is built to. When my turn comes I open with **Trip Attack** to prone the priority target, Action Surge into a full second attack sequence, and let the casters capitalize on the advantage. [[bg3:great-weapon-master]] bonus attacks chain off every kill.

## Make it yours

Want spells? **Eldritch Knight** adds [[bg3:shield]] and Misty Step to the chassis. Prefer raw numbers over control? **Champion** crits more and asks less of you. The Action-Surge core is the part I keep.
