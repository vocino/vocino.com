> **Starter notes.** These are my personal habits, not rules. They're what keeps my runs fun and un-frustrating — adjust anything that doesn't match how you like to play.

The build pages cover *what* I play. This is *how* I play — the campaign-level habits that matter more than any single character sheet.

## Difficulty

I default to **Balanced** for a first or story-focused run and **Tactician** once I know the encounters. Honour Mode is its own thing: one save, permadeath rules, and a few buffed bosses — only worth it when you already trust your party.

## Party composition

I build around **coverage, not a theme**:

- **One front-line anchor** that's happy to take hits (Fighter, Paladin).
- **One control/support** that bends fights (Cleric, or a [[bg3:hold-person]] caster).
- **One burst dealer** for priority targets (Sorcerer, Rogue archer).
- **One flex slot** for utility — stealth, dialogue, or a second blaster.

Charisma lives on whoever talks. I keep a reliable concentration buff like [[bg3:bless]] somewhere in every party.

## The long-rest economy

Long rests are **cheap and you should take them** — they advance companion stories and refresh everything. I camp whenever I've burned my big slots, not on a timer. Short rests are for topping off Warlock slots and Fighter resources between fights in the same area.

## Picking your fights

Not every fight is worth taking head-on:

- **Set the terrain first.** [[bg3:pass-without-trace]], high ground, and a surprise round win more fights than stats do.
- **Open from stealth** whenever the party allows it.
- **Some fights are skippable** — talk, sneak, or shove your way past anything that risks a 40-hour save for no reward.

## A few habits that save runs

- Carry **scrolls of [[bg3:revivify]]** and elixirs at all times.
- Keep one character trained in **Sleight of Hand + Perception** for traps and locks.
- Save before every conversation that looks like it might turn into a fight.

## Make it yours

If you'd rather roleplay than optimize, ignore half of this and follow the story — the game is generous. This is just the scaffolding I lean on so the hard fights stay fun instead of frustrating.
