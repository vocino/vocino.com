> **Starter notes.** This is how I run Paladin right now — a personal default, not a tier-list answer. Numbers are point-buy baselines I tweak per run, and I keep editing as I find new toys. Treat it as my notes, then make it yours.

A **Half-Elf Oath of Vengeance** lead built to do one thing extremely well: walk up to the scariest thing on the field and erase it with a crit [[bg3:divine-smite]]. Charisma carries both the damage aura and every conversation, so this is also my party face.

## Why I run it

- **Front-loaded power.** Smites come online immediately, so Act 1 is never a slog.
- **One stat, two jobs.** Charisma feeds saving-throw auras *and* dialogue — no wasted points.
- **Reliable burst.** Pair a crit source with smite slots and you one-round most named enemies.

## Level path

| Level | Pick | Why |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1–2 | Paladin | Smite + Lay on Hands online early |
| 3 | Oath of Vengeance | Vow of Enmity = built-in advantage |
| 4 | Feat — Ability Improvement (CHA) | Push CHA toward 18 |
| 5 | Extra Attack | Two swings doubles smite chances |
| 6 | Aura of Protection | Party-wide save buff scales with CHA |
| 8 | Feat — [[bg3:great-weapon-master]] *or* ASI | Damage spike vs. raw reliability |

## Feats I take

- [[bg3:great-weapon-master|Great Weapon Master]] — if I commit to a two-hander.
- Ability Improvement (Charisma) — almost always my first feat.

## Spells & actions

- [[bg3:divine-smite|Divine Smite]] — the whole point; dump slots on crits.
- [[bg3:bless|Bless]] — quietly the best concentration spell in the game.
- [[bg3:command|Command]] — drop a boss prone, then smite the crit.

## Gear by act

- **Act 1:** [[bg3:phalar-aluve|Phalar Aluve]] for the Shriek damage rider.
- **Act 2:** Any heavy armor that frees concentration; smite damage riders.
- **Act 3:** [[bg3:birthright|Birthright]] for +2 CHA — better auras and better dialogue.

## How I play it

Open with Vow of Enmity on the priority target, attack to land a hit, then spend the biggest slot I have on the smite. With Extra Attack I'm fishing for a crit on the second swing before committing the slot. Aura of Protection means I park the whole party near me in any save-heavy fight.

## Make it yours

Swap Oath of Vengeance for **Oathbreaker** if you want the aura-of-hate damage and a darker tone, or multiclass a few levels of **Warlock/Sorcerer** for a charisma-SAD caster hybrid. The bones — Charisma, smites, Aura of Protection — stay the same.
