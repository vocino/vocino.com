> **Gunslinger notes.** Damiane unlocks in Chapter 3. I run pistol and musket as mains, rapier when something gets in my grill. Still tuning this as I push past Howling Hill.

Chapter 3 **Damiane** with [[cd:spencer-pistol|Spencer Pistol]] and [[cd:bekker-musket|Bekker Musket]]. She hits lighter per swing than Kliff but moves twice as much — which is the whole point. I'm trying not to stand still long enough to die.

## Why I run it

- **Pistol marks, musket deletes.** [[cd:focused-shot|Focused Shot]] turns the Spencer into burst damage; the musket opens fights from range.
- **Mobility is the armor.** Quick-step, [[cd:evasive-shot|Evasive Shot]], propeller height — if I'm standing still I'm doing it wrong.
- **Support melee exists.** [[cd:white-wind-rapier|White Wind Rapier]] and shield when I'm out of stamina or someone closes distance.

## Main weapon — Pistol

The [[cd:spencer-pistol]] is the workhorse. Higher ATK than my starter rapier and fast enough to weave between melee strings.

| | |
| --- | --- |
| Weapon | [[cd:spencer-pistol]] |
| Refinement | L4 when I can afford it |
| Role | Mid-range pressure, [[cd:focused-shot|Focused Shot]] burst |

### How I use it

1. Aim into [[cd:focused-shot|Focused Shot]] mode.
2. Light attack to **mark** targets while aiming.
3. Release aim for the **volley** on everything marked.
4. [[cd:focused-evasive-shot|Focused Evasive Shot]] when I need marks without eating a hit.

Don't treat it like a plink gun. The marks are the damage — skipping the release is just making noise.

## Main weapon — Musket

The [[cd:bekker-musket]] is the opener, not the rotation. Charge time means you need space and a breath before the pack reaches you.

| | |
| --- | --- |
| Weapon | [[cd:bekker-musket]] |
| Refinement | L4 |
| Role | Fight start, stagger, weak-spot burst |

### How I use it

- Start encounters from elevation or max range when I can.
- Charge → aim at a **weak spot** or the biggest target.
- [[cd:focused-charged-shot|Focused Charged Shot]] at L3 slows time while aiming — worth the Spirit investment.
- Swap to pistol or rapier once they're on top of me. Musket mid-scrum is how I die.

### Stat priorities

- **Attack speed** and **crit** — percentage gains on fast weapons add up.
- **Stamina** — quick-steps and aerial stuff eat the bar faster than Kliff.
- **Move speed** — Haste on boots; don't heavy-armor her.

Socket **Swift** and **Destruction** Abyss cores on weapons when Sylvia has slots open. Defense cores are a band-aid for bad dodging.

## Combos

My gunslinger loop for most Chapter 3 fights:

1. **Musket opener** — charged shot or [[cd:focused-charged-shot|Focused Charged Shot]] on the biggest target.
2. **Pistol burst** — marks, release volley, [[cd:evasive-shot|Evasive Shot]] to create space.
3. **Rapier string** if they're still up — light chain, [[cd:sword-flurry|Sword Flurry]] when Spirit allows.
4. **Repeat**, swapping to claymore if three+ enemies are hugging me.

Izuna Drop is a **finisher**, not an opener. Weaken first, then the pile-driver.

## Stamina & mobility

[[cd:flight|Flight]] on the propeller is not just exploration fluff — height fixes musket sight lines and drops aerial hits on groups below.

Quick-step recovers faster than Kliff's roll. Chain dodges, shoot on the way out, don't block-and-tank.

If the bar empties mid-fight, I'm a sitting duck. [[cd:stamina|Stamina]] in the blue tree and pacing dodges matter more here than on Kliff.

## Mistakes I keep making

<ol class="cd-mistakes">
<li>Musket in melee. Charge needs space — I still panic-shoot at arm's length.</li>
<li>Skipping pistol marks. Light attacks while aiming are the whole burst; plinking without release wastes time.</li>
<li>Standing still to aim. Quick-step or [[cd:evasive-shot|Evasive Shot]] while marking — Damiane is not a turret.</li>
<li>Opening with Izuna Drop. Startup gets punished; save it for stagger.</li>
<li>Ignoring food. Less HP than Kliff means fewer free mistakes.</li>
</ol>
